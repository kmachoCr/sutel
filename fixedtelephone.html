Fixed Telephone Lines
The subscriptions to the traditional basic telephone lines have dropped, thus impacting the fixed telephone service; this is the opposite of what happens with other telecommunication services, referred to in this report.

The number of subscriptions to the traditional basic telephone service experienced a 12% decrease in the period from 2010 through 2013, in which it went from 1,060,361 to 936,035 subscribers; whereas, subscriptions to the service of Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) grew exponentially, from 105 to 32,424. As plainly seen, the traditional basic telephone service lost 124,326 subscriptions, but the VoIP service won 32,319 new subscribers. Nevertheless, this number is insufficient to make up for the fall of total subscribers in the fixed telephone service.

The VoIP service started off as a service primarily oriented to business and corporate clients, but ever since the end of 2011, it began to strengthen its focus on the residential segment, especially through triple-play packages (subscription TV, Internet Access and VoIP).
