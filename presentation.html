Telecommunications Market
In compliance with the standing legal obligations, the Superintendence of Telecommunications and the General Directorate General for Markets (Dirección General de Mercados) have presented the Second Statistical Report 2010-2013 of the Telecommunication Sector in Costa Rica.

The methodology developed not only has resulted in the definition of a strategy for the management, collection, processing and generation of results, but it also has brought forth the improvement of collection mechanisms related to suppliers and operators. This process has been availed by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU), and was presented at the 11th World Telecommunication / ICT Indicators Symposium (WTIS), which took place in Mexico City, from 4 to 6 December 2013.

These statistics from the telecommunication industry are an instrument for operators, Telecommunication Service Providers, scholars, statesmen, government authorities, the Superintendence of Telecommunications and Costa Rican citizens at large to back up their decisions, as they show the development of the telecommunications market with reliable, objective and transparent data, which can be used to conduct their analyzes, research, studies and forecasts.

From 2010 through 2013, the telecommunications market has nearly tripled, and as of today it has become a market that represented 2.4 % of the Gross Domestic Product (GDP) in 2013. Similarly, in the same period, the workforce directly related to the sector grew by over 33 %.These statistics show that one of the main objectives of the General Telecommunications Law (Ley General de Telecomunicaciones) has been achieved: making the Telecommunication Development Index in Costa Rica grow to be similar to those of First World Countries.This report has incorporated a new chapter, which depicts the behavior of the rates for the different telecommunication services available to the public. This section shows the rates trending downwards, in most of the services. A special emphasis is provided on fixed internet access services for the home segment, as well as for Small and Medium Enterprises, where prices fell 49.6 % over the past four years in comparison to the maximum valid prices since 2009.

On the other hand, rates of up to CR₡ 18.00 per minute have been registered in the voice services for mobile phones within the same network, which represents a 40 % decrease of the Maximum Valid Price or Rate Cap.

When observing the behavior of the Average Revenue per User (ARPU), we conclude that throughout these years, the telecommunications services have been made readily available to the most deprived social strata of Costa Rican society. This has allowed for the socialization of telecommunication services;therefore, today a large portion of the Costa Rican population has access to telecommunication services, thus guaranteeing the citizens’ right to obtain telecommunication services.

I appreciate all the effort, the positive attitude and the willingness to collaborate in the creation of this second statistical report by the operators and Telecommunication Service Providers, as they have made ​​it possible to concrete this report.

Walther Herrera
Encargado, Dirección General de Mercados
