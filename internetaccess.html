Internet Access
The total subscriptions to Internet Access Services experienced an increase of 308 % during the 2011-2013 period, mobile internet being the main cause for this result.

Fixed wired and fixed wireless internet connection services closed 2013 with 474,433 y 10,450 subscribers each, showing growth rates of 25 % and 168 % compared to 2010. While the Mobile Internet Service increased its share in the total subscriptions to Internet Access Services, growing from 61 % to 88 %, fixed technologies (both, wired and wireless) saw their share reduced in the same amount.

The xDSL is the Fixed Wired Technology that has the largest number of subscribers;as an average, it possesses a 57 % share. On the other hand, WiMax was the predominant choice of service in the case of Fixed Wireless Internet Access Services, with an average of 88 %.

In the case of mobile access, cell phones were the predominant devices to access Mobile Internet Services, with an average of 97 % of the total subscribers during the period 2012-2013, while the remaining 3 % were data cards. Moreover, for 2013, an average of 80 % of the subscribers chose the prepaid mode to access Mobile Internet Services, compared to only 20 % in the postpaid mode.

In terms of revenue, the total revenue associated to Internet Access Services grew by 90 % during 2011-2013, reaching the sum of CR₵ 162,000,000. The Mobile Internet Services gained importance, growing from 29 % to a total of 58 % of this amount, closing year 2013 with a total of CR₵ 94,006,000 in revenue.
