Mobile Phones
During the 2011-2013 period, the total mobile voice traffic increased by 22 %, from 1.892 billion minutes in the fourth quarter of 2011 to 2.306 billion minutes in the fourth quarter of 2013. Nevertheless, this did not translate into an increase in the monthly average traffic by subscriber, which was reduced from 138 to 103 minutes.

In a similar fashion, the SMS monthly traffic showed a reduction of 55 % during 2011-2013 and the MMS traffic began to decline as of the second quarter of 2012.

Meanwhile the line item for mobile income that showed the greatest growth during t the 2011-2013 period was the Internet, which increased by 19 % compared to 4 % and 0.5 % of voice and SMS/MMS, respectively.

Furthermore, Mobile Data Traffic gained share rising from 14 %, in 2010, to 19 % of the total traffic of Internet Services in 2013. The Mobile Phone Service also shows an increase in data traffic, compared to the reductions of SMS and MMS.

In general, subscriptions to the Mobile Internet Service experienced a much more important growth in the share of total subscriptions, increasing from 61 % to 88 %. On the other hand, the wired and wireless fixed services closed year 2013 with 474,433 subscriptions and 10,450, respectively. They showed growth rates of 25 % and 168 % compared to 2010; however, that was not enough to keep the share they had previously gained as a means of Internet Access, as their share fell by the same amount in which the Mobile Access Services increased.
