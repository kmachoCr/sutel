Pricing
When it comes to the pricing of telecommunication services during 2013, the following points are important to be considered:
  • The home internet service is the most oversubscribed and suffers of low connection speeds. As a consequence, the service is provided at lower prices for associates with other client groups.
  • The current maximum rates in the year 2009 -initially set for internet services for the home segment, as well as for Small and Medium Enterprises- decreased by 49.6% over the past four years, due to the influence of the promotions applied.
  • The voice service for mobile telephones has a maximum rate, authorized by the SUTEL (Superintendence of Telecommunications); however, operators perform various promotions in order to attract new customers or retain existing. As a result, the average rate billed falls under that maximum rate. In this regard, this service shows rates of up to CR₵18 per minute for calls made within the operator’s native network, well below the value set by the SUTEL.
  • As for the Mobile Internet Service, operators are authorized to charge based on data downloaded in the prepaid mode. Given the large number of users who choose this type of service, the operators offer a wide variety of commercial offers, in which they combine the connection speed and the maximum download limit.
