General Evolution of the Sector
Five years after the adoption of the General Telecommunications Law, No. 8642, the sector performance evaluation shows positive results in Income, investment, employment, population coverage and diversification of telecommunications services. In this section, the main results are discussed with respect to the performance of the sector during the period 2010-2013. These results will be discussed in more detail for each service in each of the following sections of the document.
Composition of the available offer
By the end of 2013, 117 operators were authorized by the SUTEL (Superintendence of Telecommunications) to supply telecommunication services;one less than those that existed at the end of 2012 and 9 more than in 2011. Only those companies that were expressly authorized to trade these services, during those years, were considered for this report. Also, 7 operators who own a concession for the exploitation of the radio spectrum are part of the sample, due to its importance in each of the groups of services covered by the SUTEL. The monitoring of the sector is done through an indicator system.

In this regard, it is important to clarify that not all authorized operators and providers did carry a valid authorization to market their services in each of the analyzed periods. Furthermore, although some did have the appropriate authorization and were commercially active, were not considered by the SUTEL in the generating of indicators for the 2010-2013 period, due to the definition of services for that period. Therefore, the size of the sample for analysis and response rates for each service may vary from year to year. In this context, it is important to state that the number of operators or providers is not directly related to the amount of monitored Service Suppliers, as these can provide more than one service, which implies certain limitations when conducting cross-sectional analyzes on this topic.

For the 2013 period, 84 % of all operators (authorized, concessionaries or both) were considered. Together they represented more than 90 % of the total subscribers and income, in each of the analyzed services.
Income Generated by the Sector
The analysis of the Total income in the Sector of Telecommunication Services included only the fixed and mobile telephone services and data transfer service. It excluded Subscription TV services, because the wording of point 123 in Agreement 022-089-2011, "Revision of the Existing Tariff Listing" states the SUTEL will only regulate the transfer of information regarding the service of subscription TV and not the service itself, so it is physically impossible to separate the network percentage used to transport and generate contents.

Income generated by the telecommunications sector in 2013 amounted to CR₵ 539,766 Millions, which is 17 % higher than in 2012 (460,758) and 37 % higher than 2011.

The mobile phone service shows an increase in its relative share (5 points) and the fixed telephone service shows a 4 % decrease as a result of comparing the total shares of the registered total Income in 2013 with 2011. On the other hand, Internet Access Services show a share decrease of 2 % while Leased Lines shows a 2 points increase in its relative share.

Once the composition of the total income for the sector has been clarified, it is important to analyze the behavior of each of the items that comprise it.
Fixed Telephone Lines
In general, the Income from fixed telephone lines shows a downward trend, with a decrease of 12 % in the period 2010-2013, mainly caused by the falling of income from traditional basic telephone services, which has been declining by an average of 1 % every quarter ever since the beginning of 2010. The Income associated to VoIP services maintained an upward trend throughout the analyzed period, and although they increased their share in the total income of fixed telephone lines, it continues to be relatively low, which didn’t manage to offset the reduction of income by the traditional basic telephone services.

At the end of 2013, the income generated by VoIP services amounted to CRC 810,1 millions, with a relative share of 3,3 % in total Income associated with fixed telephone service.
Mobile Telephones and Mobile Internet
Total Income associated with the mobile telephone service increased by 8 %, from 2011 to 2013, rising from 263,653 to 285,862 CRC Millions. When evaluating mobile networks as a whole, the item that grew the most during this period was the Internet. It was increased by 19 % (average) compared to 4 % and 0.5 % of voice and SMS / MMS, respectively.
Internet (except Mobile)
The total Income associated with the Internet Access Service (fixed, both wired and wireless) grew by 90 % during 2011-2013, which amounted to 161,915 CRC Millions. In this regard, this increase would be higher if the corresponding mobile internet category was included;however, as stated before, this was considered as part of the mobile phone service. This clarification should be taken into account when analyzing each section of this report, because Income figures associated to Mobile Data were incorporated into the results of Mobile Phones, as well as Data Transfer.
Leased Lines
Income generated by the Leased Lines Service reflects an upward trend in the period 2011-2013. Specifically during 2011-2012, it increased by a 52 points;whereas, for the period 2012-2013, it grew 21 %, consistently, along with the linear increase of the connections.
Total Investment
In the period 2010-2012, total investment in the sector saw an increase of 1 % in regards to the Gross Domestic Product (GDP). Specifically, the period of 2010-2011 showed the greatest increase, in which investment rose from 0.8 % to 2.1 % in regard to the GDP. This was expected, considering that by the end of 2011, new mobile operators started working. These operators have made significant investments to achieve the deployment of their networks and the necessary preparation of infrastructure and other resources, in order to penetrate the Costa Rican market.

Similarly, an increase of 0.3 % of the total investment was recorded between 2011 and 2012. This was justified by the deployment of telecommunication infrastructure undertaken by operators in each sector of the market, due to a more dynamic and competitive environment in the mobile and data transfer market.

Finally, in 2013, the investment in the telecommunication declines to a 1 %, compared to a 2.4 % in 2010.
Human Capital Employed
The human resources employed in the telecommunications sector increased by 33 %;growing from 7,835 in 2010 to 10,442 employees in 2013. Period 2010-2011 registered the largest increase (23 %). Such behavior, as in the case of investment, responds to the changes experienced by the sector as part of the opening process.

Human resources employed in the telecommunications sector represent 0.5 % of the total Economically Active Population (EAP) and 0.2 % of the total population.

When the human resources employed in the telecommunications sector are analyzed by gender, the results show that 98 % are men and only 2 % are women.
